His Return

The garden feels emptier without him. Doubt whispers — perhaps you were foolish to believe, perhaps this was nothing more than a fleeting dream.

But then — footsteps. He is there again, his breath uneven as though he hurried, and in his hand rests a delicate flower plucked from the royal garden. Its petals glisten with dew beneath the lantern light.

“Forgive me, [Name],” he says softly, his voice tinged with relief. “I couldn’t let this night end unfinished. I wanted to return to you — with something, however small. This flower… it is yours.”

He extends it with trembling sincerity — and for the first time, the night feels like it truly belongs to both of you.
