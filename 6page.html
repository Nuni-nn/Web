The garden feels emptier without him. Doubt whispers—perhaps you were foolish to believe him, perhaps this was nothing more than a game.

But then, footsteps return. He is there again, his breath uneven as though he hurried, and in his hand rests a delicate flower plucked from the royal garden. Its petals glisten with dew beneath the lantern light.

— “Forgive me, [Name],” he says softly, his voice tinged with relief. “I couldn’t let this night end unfinished. I wanted to return to you—with something, however small. This flower… it is yours.”

He extends it with trembling sincerity.
